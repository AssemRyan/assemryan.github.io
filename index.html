Selected Project

Medical Robot Gripper

A quick-change concept for robotic surgical instruments, developed with a focus on cleanability, reduced component complexity and suitability for medical environments.

The Challenge

Conventional industrial tool-changing concepts are often not suitable for medical environments because cleaning, sterilization and safe handling require additional design considerations.

My Approach

The project explored a simplified mechanical concept intended to reduce unnecessary complexity and support easier cleaning while maintaining the required tool-changing function.

Focus Areas

Product development, mechanical design, design for manufacturing, medical robotics, cleanability and sterilization considerations.

Current Status

Further technical development, simulation and intellectual property evaluation are ongoing.

Automation Project

Nuevo 35 Test Bench Automation

Improvement of the stop behavior of a test-bench control program using NI LabVIEW.

Initial Situation

Pressing Stop immediately terminated the program without a controlled deceleration, creating a potential safety risk.

Goal

Implement a smooth, user-defined shutdown sequence instead of an abrupt stop.

Implemented Solution

The LabVIEW logic was extended with a ramp-down sequence, controlled voltage calculations, stop-state handling and a restart lockout during deceleration.

Result

The test bench now shuts down to zero within a configurable time, while restart is only permitted after complete standstill.

Project gallery

Key outcomes
  • Controlled shutdown to zero within a user-defined time.
  • Correct voltage calculations maintained during deceleration.
  • Restart blocked until the test bench reaches complete standstill.

Result and demonstration

The video shows the controlled ramp-down sequence and confirms that restart remains blocked until the test bench reaches complete standstill.
